What Goes In

Accepted & Prohibited Materials

Most construction debris and household junk is accepted. Some materials require special handling.

✅ Generally Accepted

  • Household furniture & junk
  • Construction & demolition debris
  • Drywall, wood, flooring
  • Roofing shingles
  • Yard waste & landscaping debris
  • Concrete & brick (check with provider)
  • Appliances (check for Freon removal)

⛔ Generally Not Accepted

  • Hazardous waste (chemicals, solvents)
  • Paint cans (even dried paint varies)
  • Motor oil & automotive fluids
  • Batteries (automotive & household)
  • Tires
  • Electronics (TVs, computers)
  • Medical / biohazardous waste

Always confirm accepted materials with your specific provider at the time of booking.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Most homeowners choose a 10 or 20 yard dumpster. A 10-yard handles a small cleanout; 20-yard covers most kitchen or bathroom remodels. For whole-home remodels or large landscaping jobs, 30 or 40 yards is usually necessary. If you're unsure, select "Not Sure" in our quote form and a local provider can help you decide.
Most household junk, construction debris, furniture, yard waste, and roofing shingles are accepted. Hazardous materials, paint, tires, electronics, and certain liquids are typically prohibited. Always confirm with your specific provider before loading.
Standard rental periods are typically 7–14 days. Many providers offer extensions at an additional daily or weekly rate. Ask about the extension policy when you receive your quote.
Permits are generally not required for dumpsters placed entirely on private property (your driveway or yard). Street placement may require a city or county permit. Your provider should be familiar with local regulations and can advise you at booking.
Weight limits vary by size and provider, typically ranging from 1–8 tons. Overage fees apply if you exceed the included tonnage allowance. Heavy materials like concrete, dirt, and brick count quickly—consider asking about a separate small container for dense debris.
Usually yes, but heavy debris fills your weight allowance fast. Providers may restrict these to smaller containers (10-yard) or charge higher per-ton rates for heavy loads. Specify your debris type in the quote form so providers can give you accurate pricing.
Providers haul debris to licensed disposal and transfer facilities. Some materials may be recycled or sorted. Ask your provider about their disposal practices if sustainability or documentation is a priority for your project.
Overfilled dumpsters—debris piled above the top rim—are a safety hazard and may not be legally transported on public roads. Providers may charge an extra fee, remove excess material at your expense, or delay pickup. Fill to the top edge only, and call for a swap-out if you need more capacity.
