Approximately 12 ft × 8 ft × 3.5 ft. Ideal for single-room cleanouts, garage decluttering, and small bathroom remodels. Fits easily in most Mullins residential driveways.

Local Regulations

Permits & Regulations in Mullins / Marion County

Dumpster Placement

Placing a dumpster on private property typically requires no placement permit. Street placement may need city or county approval.

Marion County Building Permits

Mullins is in Marion County. The county requires a building permit for any project on commercial or residential buildings where the value exceeds $1,000 and/or the work area exceeds 200 square feet. You'll need a zoning compliance permit (issued Tuesdays and Thursdays only), a signed contract between contractor and homeowner, and a current Marion County business license. The building department only accepts cash or check.

Delivery Tips

Delivery & Placement Tips for Mullins

Placement Requirements

Roll-off trucks need approximately 60 feet of straight-line space and 23 feet of vertical clearance. Choose a firm, level surface.

Protecting Your Driveway

Place plywood boards (¾-inch thick) under contact points to prevent cracking on paved surfaces.

Mullins-Specific Tips

Many properties in Mullins have older driveways and mature tree canopy, especially in the neighborhoods near downtown and the historic tobacco market area. A 10 or 15-yard dumpster may be more practical in tighter spaces. For rural Marion County properties accessed by long driveways or private roads, confirm access details with your provider ahead of time.

Loading Tips

Load heavy items first, spread evenly. Walk large items through the rear door. Fill gaps with smaller debris. Never stack above sidewalls.

What Goes In

What You Can & Cannot Throw in a Mullins SC Dumpster

Generally Accepted

  • Household junk, furniture, and appliances (doors removed from refrigerators)
  • Construction and demolition debris (drywall, lumber, siding)
  • Roofing shingles and underlayment
  • Concrete, brick, and stone (weight charges may apply)
  • Yard waste, brush, and tree limbs
  • Carpet, tile, and flooring
  • General household cleanout items

Not Accepted (Prohibited Items)

  • Hazardous materials (paint, solvents, chemicals, pesticides)
  • Batteries and electronics (e-waste)
  • Tires (contact Marion County Landfill)
  • Medical waste and pharmaceuticals
  • Asbestos-containing materials
  • Flammable liquids and propane tanks
  • Household garbage in bags (some providers restrict this)

When in doubt, ask your provider before loading.
