Chesterfield County Landfill Guide: Hours, Drop-Off Sites & Accepted Materials

Everything residents and contractors in Chesterfield County need to know about waste disposal — 7 convenience center locations, hours, accepted materials, tire and e-waste drop-off, and what you can't put in a dumpster.

Chesterfield County doesn't operate a traditional public-access landfill the way some neighboring Pee Dee counties do. Instead, the county runs 7 convenience centers spread across the county where residents can drop off household waste, recyclables, electronics, tires, and used motor oil. Before you load up a truck or rent a dumpster, here's what you need to know about each location, what's accepted, and what's not.

Chesterfield County Convenience Centers

Chesterfield County Solid Waste & Recycling

County Recycling Coordinator: Rose Gainey

Office Address: 97 Jones Road, Chesterfield, SC 29709

Phone: (843) 623-2464

Hours (all 7 centers): Tuesday, Thursday & Saturday, 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM

Closed: Monday, Wednesday, Friday, and Sunday. Contact the Recycling Coordinator for holiday closures.

All convenience centers accept household waste, recyclables, scrap metal, electronics, and used motor oil (5-gallon residential limit). Tire drop-off is limited to three specific locations — see details below.

All 7 Convenience Center Locations

Chesterfield County's convenience centers are distributed across the county so most residents are within a short drive of at least one site. All locations share the same hours: Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday, 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

Accepted Materials at Chesterfield County Convenience Centers

All 7 convenience centers accept the following materials from Chesterfield County residents:

Standard Accepted Items

  • Household trash and bagged waste
  • Cardboard (flattened)
  • Newspapers, magazines, and paper
  • Plastic bottles (#1 and #2)
  • Aluminum and tin cans
  • Scrap metal and steel (excluding white goods/appliances at some locations)
  • Yard waste and brush
  • Paperboard canisters (coffee cans, Pringles containers, dough canisters)

Special Items (With Restrictions)

  • Used motor oil — 5-gallon maximum per visit; residential only (businesses cannot bring in oil)
  • Oil filters — must be pierced and drained for 24 hours; maximum 10 filters per visit
  • Batteries — used automotive, marine, and lawn mower batteries only
  • Tires — accepted only at Chesterfield, Jefferson, and Patrick centers; must be off the rim
  • Electronics (e-waste) — TVs, computers, monitors, printers, and other electronic equipment accepted at all locations

Prohibited Items — Chesterfield County Convenience Centers & Dumpsters

These items are not accepted at Chesterfield County convenience centers or in standard roll-off dumpsters:

Hazardous Household Waste

  • Liquid paint, stains, and varnishes
  • Pesticides and herbicides
  • Pool chemicals and cleaning solvents
  • Compressed gas cylinders and propane tanks

Other Prohibited Items

  • Medical and biohazardous waste
  • Asbestos-containing materials
  • Free liquids of any kind
  • Commercial or industrial waste oil (residential only — 5-gallon limit)

Items Accepted at Convenience Centers But Not in Dumpsters

  • Tires — accepted at Chesterfield, Jefferson, and Patrick centers only; not in roll-off containers
  • Electronics (e-waste) — accepted at all convenience centers; not in roll-off containers
  • Motor oil & filters — take to a convenience center (5-gallon / 10-filter limit); not in dumpsters
  • Batteries — automotive and marine batteries accepted at convenience centers; not in dumpsters
Warning: Disposing of prohibited items in a roll-off dumpster can result in load rejection, additional fees, and fines under South Carolina solid waste regulations. When in doubt, call your provider before loading.

E-Waste Drop-Off in Chesterfield County

All 7 Chesterfield County convenience centers accept household electronics during their regular operating hours — no special event or appointment required:

  • Accepted electronics: Televisions, computers, computer monitors, printers, and other electronic equipment
  • Hours: Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday, 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM
  • Cost: No charge for Chesterfield County residents

For large quantities of electronics or commercial e-waste, contact the County Recycling Coordinator at (843) 623-2464 before making a trip.

Environmental Compliance for Chesterfield County Contractors

Contractors working in Chesterfield County must dispose of construction and demolition waste at licensed facilities. The county's convenience centers are designed for residential use — contractors with large C&D loads should use a licensed roll-off provider or contact the county about commercial disposal options.

Key Rules for Contractors

  • Keep a waste manifest for commercial C&D loads
  • Never mix hazardous materials (paint, solvents, chemicals) with general debris
  • All loads must be covered during transport under SC law — violations carry fines
  • Use a licensed roll-off provider for job site waste to ensure compliant disposal without multiple trips
  • Contact Chesterfield County Building Inspections for permit requirements before starting work

When a Dumpster Makes More Sense Than a Convenience Center Trip

For a few bags of household trash or a small load of yard debris, a convenience center visit makes sense — it's free for Chesterfield County residents and there are 7 locations across the county. But Chesterfield County's convenience centers are only open three days a week, which means timing your project around Tuesdays, Thursdays, and Saturdays.

For renovation projects, full cleanouts, roofing tear-offs, or any job that generates more than a truck bed of debris, a roll-off dumpster is the practical choice. Your provider handles transport, disposal, and compliance — no scheduling around limited hours, no load-securing hassle, no per-trip driving time across rural roads.

For contractors running job sites in Chesterfield, Cheraw, Pageland, or McBee, roll-off rentals also eliminate the need to track individual disposal runs and ensure every load reaches a licensed facility.
