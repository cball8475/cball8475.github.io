Sumter SC Landfill Guide: Prohibited Items and Recycling Centers

Everything Sumter County residents and contractors need to know about proper waste disposal, recycling drop-off locations, and staying compliant with SC regulations.

Before you load that dumpster or make a trip to the landfill, there's something every Sumter County resident and contractor needs to know — not everything can go in a roll-off container or standard landfill. South Carolina has specific regulations around waste disposal, and violating them can result in fines or rejected loads.

Sumter County Solid Waste Facility

🏢 Sumter County Landfill & Transfer Station

Address: 2185 East Brewington Road, Sumter, SC 29153

Phone: (803) 495-3317

Type: Class II C&D Landfill — construction and demolition debris accepted. Municipal solid waste (MSW) handled through an on-site Waste Management transfer station.

Tipping Fee: $41/ton (MSW transfer station)

Accepts construction and demolition debris at the Class II C&D landfill. MSW, household waste, and bulk items go through the Waste Management transfer station on-site.

Prohibited Items at Sumter County Landfill

The following items are not accepted at the Sumter County landfill and cannot be placed in standard roll-off dumpsters:

Hazardous Household Waste

  • Paint (liquid) and solvents
  • Pesticides and herbicides
  • Motor oil and automotive fluids
  • Compressed gas cylinders

Other Prohibited Items

  • Tires
  • Car and truck batteries
  • Medical and biohazardous waste
  • Asbestos-containing materials
  • Liquid waste of any kind
⚠️ Warning: Disposing of prohibited items at the Sumter County landfill or in a roll-off dumpster can result in load rejection, additional fees, and fines under South Carolina solid waste regulations.

E-Waste Drop-Off in Sumter SC

Sumter County offers two options for electronics disposal — neither requires a special event or advance registration:

  • Sumter County Landfill (2185 East Brewington Rd) — E-waste accepted Monday–Saturday, 7:30 AM to 4:00 PM.
  • City of Sumter Drop-Off (1289 N. Main Street) — First Saturday of every month, 8:00 AM to noon.

Accepted e-waste typically includes televisions, monitors, computers, laptops, printers, and small electronics. Call (803) 495-3317 to confirm what's accepted before making the trip.

Sumter County Recycling Centers

Sumter County operates 9 drop-off recycling centers serving residents across the county. These are for residential use only — commercial haulers are not permitted.

📦 Recycling Center Hours

Monday, Wednesday, Friday, Saturday: 7:00 AM – 7:00 PM

Sunday: 1:30 PM – 7:00 PM

Closed: Tuesday and Thursday

Residents only. No commercial accounts.

Standard accepted materials include cardboard, paper, plastic bottles (#1 and #2), glass, and aluminum and steel cans.

On-Site Processing at the Landfill

The Sumter County landfill facility includes on-site wood grinding and cardboard bailing operations. Clean wood waste and cardboard from large cleanouts may qualify — call ahead to confirm current acceptance.

Environmental Compliance for Sumter County Contractors

Contractors working in Sumter County must ensure C&D waste is disposed of at licensed C&D facilities. The Sumter County landfill is a Class II C&D facility — appropriate for construction debris, clean concrete, wood waste, and drywall from permitted projects. Illegal dumping carries significant fines under South Carolina law.

Best Practices for Contractors

  • Keep a waste manifest for large C&D loads
  • Separate materials on-site — recyclables, clean wood, and heavy debris in separate containers reduces disposal costs
  • Never mix hazardous materials with general construction waste
  • Use a licensed dumpster rental provider who disposes at approved Sumter County facilities
  • Confirm debris type is accepted at the Class II C&D facility before hauling — MSW goes through the Waste Management transfer station, not the same gate
