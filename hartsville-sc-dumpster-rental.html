Choosing the Right Dumpster Size

Picking the right size saves money. Too small and you'll need a second haul. Too large and you're paying for unused space. Here's how each size maps to common Hartsville-area projects:

10 Yard Dumpster

Approximately 12 ft × 8 ft × 3.5 ft. Ideal for single-room cleanouts, garage decluttering, and small bathroom remodels. Fits easily in Hartsville's older neighborhood driveways near the Coker University campus and downtown area where lot sizes can be tighter.

15 Yard Dumpster

Approximately 14 ft × 8 ft × 4 ft. A solid middle ground for medium cleanouts, carpet removal, and moderate renovation projects. Popular with Hartsville homeowners doing seasonal purges or single-room remodels.

20 Yard Dumpster

Approximately 22 ft × 8 ft × 4 ft. The most popular size across Darlington County. Handles kitchen gut-outs, deck removals, full-floor flooring replacement, and mid-size estate cleanouts. Fits standard residential driveways with room to spare.

30 Yard Dumpster

Approximately 22 ft × 8 ft × 6 ft. Built for full home remodels, large roofing tear-offs, and major cleanouts. Common on Hartsville construction sites and larger residential renovation projects. Taller sidewalls give more capacity without a bigger footprint.

40 Yard Dumpster

Approximately 22 ft × 8 ft × 8 ft. The largest available — reserved for major construction, commercial demolition, and large-scale property cleanouts. Requires adequate clearance and a firm, level surface. Contractors working on commercial projects along US-15 or Fourth Street often need this size.

Permits & Regulations in Hartsville, SC

Dumpster Placement Permits

Placing a dumpster on your own property — driveway, yard, or private parking area — typically requires no permit in Hartsville. If you need to place the container on a public street, sidewalk, or right-of-way, contact the City of Hartsville to check requirements.

Building & Construction Permits

The City of Hartsville follows the 2015 International Property Maintenance Code (as adopted by City Council in Ordinance 4291). Building permits and business licenses for construction work are handled through the Codes & Licensing Office. Contractors need a current business license to operate within city limits.

Delivery Tips

Delivery & Placement Tips for Hartsville

Placement Requirements

Roll-off trucks need approximately 60 feet of straight-line space and 23 feet of vertical clearance. Choose a firm, level surface — concrete driveways work best. Avoid soft ground, especially during wet weather common in the Pee Dee region, as the weight can damage lawns and create ruts.

Protecting Your Driveway

Place plywood boards (at least ¾-inch thick) under the contact points to distribute weight and prevent cracking. Your provider may supply boards — ask when confirming delivery.

Before Delivery Day

Move vehicles and obstacles from the delivery path. Trim overhanging branches. If a gate needs opening, ensure someone is available. You don't have to be home if the area is accessible and clear.

Hartsville-Specific Tips

Many homes in Hartsville's historic neighborhoods near Coker University and along Home Avenue have narrower driveways and mature tree canopy. A 10 or 15-yard dumpster is often more practical in these areas than a full 30-yard. If you're on a tree-lined street, confirm vertical clearance with your provider before delivery.

Loading Tips

Load heavy items first and spread them evenly. Walk large items in through the swinging rear door. Fill gaps with smaller debris. Never stack above the sidewalls — overfilled containers can't be safely transported.

What Goes In

What You Can & Cannot Throw in a Hartsville SC Dumpster

Generally Accepted

  • Household junk, furniture, and appliances (doors removed from refrigerators)
  • Construction and demolition debris (drywall, lumber, siding)
  • Roofing shingles and underlayment
  • Concrete, brick, and stone (weight charges may apply)
  • Yard waste, brush, and tree limbs
  • Carpet, tile, and flooring
  • General household cleanout items

Not Accepted (Prohibited Items)

  • Hazardous materials (paint, solvents, chemicals, pesticides)
  • Batteries and electronics (e-waste)
  • Tires (must go to the Darlington County Landfill directly)
  • Medical waste and pharmaceuticals
  • Asbestos-containing materials
  • Flammable liquids and propane tanks
  • Household garbage in bags (some providers restrict this)

When in doubt, ask your provider before loading. Prohibited items can result in additional fees or rejection at the landfill.
