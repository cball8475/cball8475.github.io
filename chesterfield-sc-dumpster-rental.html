Delivery Tips

Delivery & Placement Tips for Chesterfield

Placement Requirements

Roll-off trucks need approximately 60 feet of straight-line space and 23 feet of vertical clearance to safely place a dumpster. Choose a firm, level surface — concrete driveways work best. The sandy soil common in the Sandhills region of Chesterfield County can shift under heavy loads, so avoid placing containers on bare sand or loose gravel.

Protecting Your Driveway

Dumpsters are heavy, even before you fill them. Place plywood boards (at least ¾-inch thick) under the contact points to distribute weight and prevent cracking or scraping on your driveway surface. Your provider may supply boards — ask when you confirm your delivery.

Before Delivery Day

Move vehicles, bicycles, and any obstacles out of the delivery path. Trim overhanging tree branches that might obstruct the truck. If a gate needs to be opened, make sure someone is available to let the driver in. You don't have to be home during delivery as long as the area is accessible and clear.

Loading Tips

Load heavy items first (concrete, dirt, lumber) and spread them evenly across the bottom. Walk large items in through the swinging rear door. Fill gaps with smaller debris. Never stack materials above the sidewalls — overfilled containers can't be transported safely and your provider may charge an additional fee.

What Goes In

What You Can & Cannot Throw in a Chesterfield SC Dumpster

Generally Accepted

  • Household junk, furniture, and appliances (doors removed from refrigerators)
  • Construction and demolition debris (drywall, lumber, siding)
  • Roofing shingles and underlayment
  • Concrete, brick, and stone (note: weight charges may apply)
  • Yard waste, brush, and tree limbs
  • Carpet, tile, and flooring
  • General household cleanout items

Not Accepted (Prohibited Items)

  • Hazardous materials (paint, solvents, chemicals, pesticides)
  • Batteries and electronics (e-waste)
  • Tires (accepted only at the Chesterfield, Jefferson, and Patrick convenience centers)
  • Medical waste and pharmaceuticals
  • Asbestos-containing materials
  • Flammable liquids and propane tanks
  • Business/commercial garbage (not accepted at convenience centers)

When in doubt, ask your provider before loading. Prohibited items can result in additional disposal fees or rejection at the landfill. For specialty waste disposal options in Chesterfield County, see the landfill and recycling section below.

What the Recycling Centers Accept

All 7 centers accept household garbage (must be bagged), cardboard, paperboard, white goods (stoves, washers, dryers), scrap metal, used automotive batteries, and used oil (5-gallon max per visit). Tires are only accepted at the Chesterfield, Jefferson, and Patrick locations. Business garbage is not accepted at convenience centers.

Chesterfield County comingles recyclables — you don't need to separate plastic bottles, aluminum cans, and cardboard. For more details, contact the Chesterfield County Solid Waste Department.
