Florence County Solid Waste Facility

Address: 1306 S. Coit St., Florence, SC 29501

Phone: (843) 665-3050

Hours: Monday–Friday 7:00 AM – 5:00 PM  |  Saturday 7:00 AM – 12:00 PM

Closed: Sundays and major holidays

Accepts household waste, construction debris, yard waste, and bulk items. Tipping fees apply for loads over standard thresholds.

Prohibited Items at Florence County Landfill

The following items are not accepted at the Florence County landfill and cannot be placed in standard roll-off dumpsters:

Hazardous Household Waste

  • Paint (liquid), stains, and varnishes
  • Pesticides, herbicides, and fertilizers
  • Cleaning solvents and pool chemicals
  • Motor oil and automotive fluids
  • Propane and compressed gas cylinders

Electronics and Appliances

  • Televisions and computer monitors
  • Refrigerators and air conditioners (contain refrigerants requiring certified removal)
  • Fluorescent bulbs and ballasts (contain mercury)

Other Prohibited Items

  • Tires (accepted at designated tire collection events only)
  • Car and truck batteries
  • Medical and biohazardous waste
  • Asbestos-containing materials
  • Liquid waste of any kind
⚠️ Warning: Disposing of prohibited items at the Florence County landfill or in a roll-off dumpster can result in load rejection, additional fees, and fines under South Carolina solid waste regulations.

Where to Recycle in Florence SC

Florence County operates drop-off recycling centers at convenient locations throughout the county. Standard accepted materials include cardboard, paper, plastic bottles (#1 and #2), glass, and aluminum and steel cans.

Environmental Compliance for Florence SC Contractors

Contractors working in Florence County must ensure C&D waste is disposed of at licensed C&D facilities. Illegal dumping carries significant fines under South Carolina law and can result in contractor license consequences.

Best Practices for Contractors

  • Keep a waste manifest for large C&D loads
  • Separate materials on-site — recyclables, clean wood, and heavy debris in separate containers reduces disposal costs
  • Never mix hazardous materials with general construction waste
  • Use a licensed dumpster rental provider who disposes at approved Florence County facilities
  • Link contractor permits to the City of Florence Public Works requirements when working in city limits
