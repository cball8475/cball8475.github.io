Choosing the Right Dumpster Size

Picking the right size saves money. Here's how each size maps to common Lake City-area projects:

10 Yard Dumpster

Approximately 12 ft × 8 ft × 3.5 ft. Ideal for single-room cleanouts, garage decluttering, and small bathroom remodels. Fits easily in most Lake City driveways.

15 Yard Dumpster

Approximately 14 ft × 8 ft × 4 ft. Great for medium cleanouts, carpet removal, and moderate renovation projects. A good fit for Lake City homeowners preparing properties for ArtFields season or doing seasonal cleanups.

20 Yard Dumpster

Approximately 22 ft × 8 ft × 4 ft. The most popular size across Florence County. Handles kitchen gut-outs, deck removals, flooring replacement, and estate cleanouts.

40 Yard Dumpster

Approximately 22 ft × 8 ft × 8 ft. The largest available — for major construction, commercial demolition, and large-scale property cleanouts. Requires adequate clearance and a firm, level surface.

Permits & Regulations in Lake City, SC

Dumpster Placement Permits

Placing a dumpster on your own property typically requires no permit. If you need to place on a public street or right-of-way, check with the City of Lake City first.

Building & Construction Permits

The Lake City Codes Enforcement Department handles building permits, zoning, and licensing for construction work within city limits. Permits may be required depending on the scope and value of your project.

Delivery Tips

Delivery & Placement Tips for Lake City

Placement Requirements

Roll-off trucks need approximately 60 feet of straight-line space and 23 feet of vertical clearance. Choose a firm, level surface — concrete driveways work best. Avoid soft ground, which is common in low-lying areas around Lake City, especially during rainy seasons.

Protecting Your Driveway

Place plywood boards (at least ¾-inch thick) under the contact points to distribute weight and prevent cracking. Ask your provider if they supply boards.

Lake City-Specific Tips

Lake City sits in a low-elevation area of the Pee Dee, so drainage matters. Avoid placing dumpsters in areas prone to standing water. If you're doing a cleanout in the downtown Main Street corridor during ArtFields season (typically April), coordinate delivery timing with your provider to avoid conflicts with event traffic and parking.

Loading Tips

Load heavy items first, spread evenly across the bottom. Walk large items through the rear door. Fill gaps with smaller debris. Never stack above sidewalls.

What Goes In

What You Can & Cannot Throw in a Lake City SC Dumpster

Generally Accepted

  • Household junk, furniture, and appliances (doors removed from refrigerators)
  • Construction and demolition debris (drywall, lumber, siding)
  • Roofing shingles and underlayment
  • Concrete, brick, and stone (weight charges may apply)
  • Yard waste, brush, and tree limbs
  • Carpet, tile, and flooring
  • General household cleanout items

Not Accepted (Prohibited Items)

  • Hazardous materials (paint, solvents, chemicals, pesticides)
  • Batteries and electronics (e-waste)
  • Tires (can be dropped off at the Lake City Municipal Landfill)
  • Medical waste and pharmaceuticals
  • Asbestos-containing materials
  • Flammable liquids and propane tanks
  • Household garbage in bags (some providers restrict this)

When in doubt, ask your provider before loading. The Lake City Municipal Landfill accepts many items that can't go in a roll-off dumpster.
