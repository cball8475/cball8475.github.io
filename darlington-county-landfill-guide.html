Darlington County Landfill Guide: Hours, Recycling Centers & Prohibited Items

Everything residents and contractors in Darlington and Hartsville need to know — free C&D disposal, 13 recycling station locations, e-waste drop-off, and what you can't put in a dumpster.

Before you haul a load to the landfill or load up a dumpster, there are a few things Darlington County residents and contractors should know. Not everything can go in a roll-off container, and not all waste goes to the same place. Here's the complete breakdown for Darlington County — including the part most people don't know: C&D disposal is free for county residents.

Darlington County Landfill

🏢 Darlington County C&D Landfill

Address: 2301 Great Cypress Road, Darlington, SC 29540

Phone: (843) 398-4800

Hours: Monday–Saturday, 8:00 AM – 4:00 PM

Closed: Sundays and county holidays. Check in at the Scale House on arrival.

Tipping Fee: No fee for Darlington County residents disposing of C&D debris. Commercial operators and licensed haulers may be subject to separate rates — confirm before arrival.

Accepts construction and demolition debris, yard waste, bulk items, tires, and electronics. All loads must be secured and covered during transport — South Carolina law requires it.

Prohibited Items — Darlington County Landfill & Dumpsters

These items are not accepted in standard roll-off dumpsters and may be restricted or require special handling at the landfill:

Hazardous Household Waste

  • Liquid paint, stains, and varnishes
  • Pesticides and herbicides
  • Pool chemicals and cleaning solvents
  • Compressed gas cylinders and propane tanks

Other Prohibited Items

  • Medical and biohazardous waste
  • Asbestos-containing materials
  • Free liquids of any kind

Items Accepted at the Landfill But Not in Dumpsters

  • Tires — accepted at the landfill directly; not at recycling centers
  • Electronics (e-waste) — accepted at the landfill Mon–Sat; not in roll-off containers
  • Motor oil — take to a recycling center (10-gallon limit per visit); not the landfill
⚠️ Warning: Disposing of prohibited items in a roll-off dumpster can result in load rejection, additional fees, and fines under South Carolina solid waste regulations. When in doubt, call your provider before loading.

E-Waste Drop-Off in Darlington County

The Darlington County Landfill accepts household electronics during its regular operating hours — no special event or appointment required:

  • Darlington County Landfill (2301 Great Cypress Road) — E-waste accepted Monday–Saturday, 8:00 AM to 4:00 PM. Accepted items include televisions, computers, monitors, printers, and other electronic equipment.

Hartsville residents: Contact the City of Hartsville Public Services Department at (843) 383-3019 (Recycling Coordinator Brad Jordan, 402 S. Leesburg Street) for information on local electronics recycling events and options.

Darlington County's 13 Recycling Stations

Darlington County operates 13 recycling drop-off stations spread across the county. All stations accept newspapers, plastic bottles (#1 and #2), cardboard, used motor oil (10-gallon limit per visit), oil filters, and textiles. Glass and styrofoam are not accepted at any recycling center. Tires must go to the main landfill.

City of Darlington Curbside Recycling

City of Darlington residents with curbside service use blue roll carts for recycling (cardboard, plastic, newspapers, magazines, paper, aluminum cans — no glass) and green carts for regular garbage. Recycling pickup runs every other week. The city does not pick up furniture or appliances from the curb — take those to one of the county's 13 recycling stations. Contact Darlington Street & Sanitation at (843) 398-4035.

Environmental Compliance for Darlington County Contractors

Contractors working in Darlington County must dispose of C&D waste at licensed facilities. The county landfill at Great Cypress Road is a licensed C&D facility — appropriate for clean concrete, wood, drywall, roofing, and general construction debris from permitted projects.

Key Rules for Contractors

  • Keep a waste manifest for commercial C&D loads
  • Never mix hazardous materials (paint, solvents, chemicals) with general debris
  • All loads must be covered during transport under SC law — violations carry fines
  • Permits required for work over $200 within Darlington city limits — contact Darlington Codes Enforcement at (843) 398-4029
  • Use a licensed roll-off provider for job site waste to ensure compliant disposal without multiple landfill trips

When a Dumpster Makes More Sense Than a Landfill Trip

For small loads — a few bags of yard debris or a single piece of furniture — the landfill makes sense, especially since Darlington County residents pay no tipping fee. But for renovation projects, full cleanouts, roofing tear-offs, or any job that generates more than a truck bed of debris, a roll-off dumpster is more practical. Your provider handles transport, disposal, and compliance — no landfill queues, no load-securing hassle, no per-trip driving time.

For contractors running multiple job sites across Darlington County, roll-off rentals also eliminate the need to track individual disposal runs and ensure every load hits a licensed facility.
