Permits & Regulations in Sumter, SC

Dumpster Placement

Placing a dumpster on private property typically requires no placement permit. Street placement may need city or county approval.

City of Sumter Building Permits

The City of Sumter Building Department handles all building permits, inspections, and code enforcement. Permits are required for construction, additions, remodeling, and demolition work within city limits. Inspection requests must be submitted before 3 PM for next-day scheduling.

Delivery Tips

Delivery & Placement Tips for Sumter

Placement Requirements

Roll-off trucks need approximately 60 feet of straight-line space and 23 feet of vertical clearance. Choose a firm, level surface — concrete driveways work best.

Protecting Your Driveway

Place plywood boards (¾-inch thick) under contact points to prevent cracking. Ask your provider if they supply boards.

Sumter-Specific Tips

Sumter's clay-heavy soil can become soft and muddy during rainy periods, making lawn placement risky. Stick to paved surfaces when possible. If you're on base housing near Shaw AFB, check with your housing office about dumpster placement policies before ordering. For properties in flood-prone areas near the Wateree River or Pocotaligo Creek, avoid placing dumpsters in low-lying spots.

Loading Tips

Load heavy items first, spread evenly. Walk large items through the rear door. Fill gaps with smaller debris. Never stack above sidewalls.

What Goes In

What You Can & Cannot Throw in a Sumter SC Dumpster

Generally Accepted

  • Household junk, furniture, and appliances (doors removed from refrigerators)
  • Construction and demolition debris (drywall, lumber, siding)
  • Roofing shingles and underlayment
  • Concrete, brick, and stone (weight charges may apply)
  • Yard waste, brush, and tree limbs
  • Carpet, tile, and flooring
  • General household cleanout items

Not Accepted (Prohibited Items)

  • Hazardous materials (paint, solvents, chemicals, pesticides)
  • Batteries and electronics (e-scrap accepted at Sumter County Landfill only)
  • Tires
  • Medical waste and pharmaceuticals
  • Asbestos-containing materials
  • Flammable liquids and propane tanks
  • Free liquids of any kind

When in doubt, ask your provider. Electronics and e-waste must go directly to the Sumter County Landfill — they have a dedicated e-scrap recycling drop-off area.
