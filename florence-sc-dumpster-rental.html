What to Expect on Delivery Day

Placement Checklist

  • Clear a space at least 10 ft wide × 22 ft long for the container footprint
  • Ensure 23 ft of overhead clearance — watch for tree branches and utility lines
  • Move any vehicles that could block the delivery truck from the street
  • Mark sprinkler heads, septic access lids, and any soft ground areas
  • If your driveway is older or cracked, ask the driver to place boards under the container feet

Day-of Communication

Most Florence providers will call 30–60 minutes before arrival. Keep your phone on. If your placement situation has changed (vehicle in the way, flooded area), contact your provider before the truck arrives to avoid trip fees.

Loading Best Practices

  • Fill heaviest items first — concrete, tile, and dirt go on the bottom
  • Break down large items like furniture and pallets to maximize space
  • Never fill above the rim — overloaded containers can't be transported legally and will be flagged
  • Keep prohibited items out: no liquids, hazardous chemicals, tires, or wet paint
