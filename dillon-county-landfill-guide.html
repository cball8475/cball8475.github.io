Dillon County Landfill Guide: Hours, 13 Drop-Off Sites & Prohibited Items

What Dillon County residents and contractors need to know — the landfill only buries C&D debris and yard waste (general trash goes to Lee County), 11 convenience centers on a Monday/Wednesday/Friday schedule, and where to take electronics, tires, and motor oil.

Dillon County's waste system works differently than most neighboring counties. General household trash is not buried locally — it's hauled to Lee County for disposal. The Dillon County Landfill at 901 Landfill Road only accepts construction and demolition debris and yard waste. Everything else runs through the county's 13-site convenience center network. Here's where to take what.

Dillon County Landfill & Transfer Station

🏢 Dillon County Landfill & Transfer Station

Address: 901 Landfill Road (off Highway 57 East), Dillon, SC 29536

Phone: (843) 774-1436

Email: Dclandfill@dilloncountysc.org

Hours: Monday–Friday, 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Closed: Saturdays, Sundays, and federal holidays.

What's buried here: C&D debris and yard waste only. General household trash is transferred to Lee County for disposal.

Also accepts tires, electronics, large appliances, cardboard, used motor oil (including farmer oil up to 55 gallons), and scrap metal. All loads must be secured and covered during transport — South Carolina law requires it.

Prohibited Items — Dillon County Landfill & Dumpsters

These items are not accepted in standard roll-off dumpsters and may be restricted or require special handling:

Hazardous Household Waste

  • Liquid paint, stains, and varnishes
  • Pesticides and herbicides
  • Pool chemicals and cleaning solvents
  • Compressed gas cylinders and propane tanks

Other Prohibited Items

  • Medical and biohazardous waste
  • Asbestos-containing materials
  • Free liquids of any kind

Items Accepted at Specific Locations Only

  • Electronics (e-waste) — only at 608 South 1st Avenue and the Landfill; not at the 11 convenience centers
  • Glass — only at 608 South 1st Avenue; not at convenience centers or the landfill
  • Mixed paper (books, magazines, office paper) — only at 608 South 1st Avenue
  • Cardboard — only at Highway 34, Highway 41, SC 34 West, South 1st Avenue, and the Landfill
  • Large appliances — accepted at convenience centers and landfill, but not at 608 South 1st Avenue
  • Farmer oil (up to 55 gallons) — landfill only
  • Used motor oil and filters — accepted at convenience centers and landfill, but not at 608 South 1st Avenue
⚠️ Warning: Not every drop-off site accepts the same materials. Check the tables below before loading up. Disposing of prohibited items in a roll-off dumpster can result in load rejection, additional fees, and fines under South Carolina solid waste regulations.

E-Waste Drop-Off in Dillon County

Dillon County accepts household electronics at only two locations:

  • Dillon County Recycling Center (608 South 1st Avenue, Dillon) — Monday–Friday, 8:00 AM – 4:00 PM
  • Dillon County Landfill (901 Landfill Road, Dillon) — Monday–Friday, 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Accepted electronics include computers, laptops, printers, televisions, and other electronic equipment. The 11 convenience centers do not accept electronics.

✅ Pro Tip: The convenience centers are only open Monday, Wednesday, and Friday. If you need to drop off on a Tuesday, Thursday, or weekend, the only options are the Recycling Center at 608 South 1st Avenue (weekdays only, closes 4 PM) or the Landfill at 901 Landfill Road (weekdays only, closes 5 PM). Plan around the schedule or you're making a wasted trip.

City of Dillon Curbside Collection

City of Dillon residents have curbside collection for household garbage and recyclables (aluminum cans, plastic bottles, cardboard, newspapers). Contact Dillon City Public Works at (843) 774-0040 for pickup schedules and rules. The city does not pick up large appliances or electronics curbside — take those to the Recycling Center or Landfill.

Environmental Compliance for Dillon County Contractors

Contractors working in Dillon County should know that only C&D debris and yard waste are buried at the local landfill. General construction waste mixed with household materials will need to go through the transfer station system, which hauls to Lee County.

Key Rules for Contractors

  • Keep a waste manifest for commercial C&D loads
  • Never mix hazardous materials (paint, solvents, chemicals) with general debris
  • All loads must be covered during transport under SC law — violations carry fines
  • The landfill is closed weekends — plan haul schedules accordingly
  • Use a licensed roll-off provider for job site waste to ensure compliant disposal without managing the transfer-to-Lee-County logistics yourself

When a Dumpster Makes More Sense Than a Landfill Trip

For a single load of yard debris or a few bags of household waste, the convenience centers and landfill work fine. But Dillon County's system adds a layer of complexity that most counties don't have — general trash doesn't stay local, the convenience centers run three days a week, and different sites accept different materials.

For renovation projects, full cleanouts, roofing tear-offs, or any job producing more than a truck bed of debris, a roll-off dumpster takes all of that off your plate. Your provider handles transport, disposal routing, and compliance — no figuring out which site takes what, no scheduling around Monday/Wednesday/Friday windows.
