Local Regulations

Permits & Regulations in Kingstree, SC

Dumpster Placement

Placing a dumpster on private property (your driveway or yard) typically requires no placement permit. If the container needs to go in the street or on public right-of-way, you'll need a permit from the Town of Kingstree before delivery.

Town of Kingstree Permits

The Town of Kingstree handles permits for any container placed in the town's right-of-way. The town enforces the 2021 Building and Fire Code editions, effective January 1, 2023.

Town of Kingstree — Permits
401 N. Longstreet Street
Kingstree, SC 29556
Website: kingstree.org/permits
💡 Easy rule: Dumpster fits on your driveway? No permit needed. Going in the street? Contact the Town first.

Building Permits for Construction Work

If your project involves construction, renovation, or demolition within town limits, a building permit is required in addition to any dumpster placement considerations. Contact the Town of Kingstree permits office before starting work.

Williamsburg County (outside town limits)

For properties outside Kingstree town limits in unincorporated Williamsburg County, contact Williamsburg County government regarding any right-of-way requirements. Most rural driveways and properties require no permit for private placement.

Delivery Tips

Delivery & Placement Tips

A few things that make delivery smooth in Williamsburg County:

Clear the drop zone

Clear a space at least 10 feet wide and 22 feet long before the driver arrives. Ensure 23 feet of overhead clearance. Move vehicles, trim low branches, and mark any utility access covers.

Protect your driveway

Ask your provider if they use boards or plywood under the container to prevent damage to paved driveways. Many rural Williamsburg County properties have gravel or packed dirt — confirm the surface is firm and level before delivery.

Rural road access

Some back roads off US-52 and SC-261 in rural Williamsburg County have weight or clearance restrictions. Confirm your road is accessible for a large delivery truck when booking.

What Goes In

What You Can & Cannot Throw in a Dumpster

Generally Accepted

Household furniture and junk, appliances (check with provider), construction debris (drywall, lumber, flooring), roofing shingles, concrete and brick, yard waste and tree branches, carpet and padding, general household cleanout items.

Prohibited Items

Hazardous materials of any kind, paint and solvents, motor oil and automotive fluids, propane tanks, batteries (car and household), electronics (TVs, computers, monitors), asbestos-containing materials, tires (unless provider specifically allows), and free liquids.

⚠️ Unsure about a specific item? Ask your provider before loading. Prohibited items can result in load rejection, additional disposal fees, and fines under South Carolina DHEC solid waste regulations.
