Size Guide

Choosing the Right Dumpster Size

Picking the right size saves money. Too small and you'll need a second haul. Too large and you're paying for space you don't use. Here's how each size maps to common Darlington-area projects:

10 Yard Dumpster

Dimensions: approximately 12 ft long × 8 ft wide × 3.5 ft high. Ideal for single-room cleanouts, garage decluttering, and small bathroom remodels. This is the smallest roll-off available and fits easily in most Darlington driveways, including tighter lots near the historic downtown area.

15 Yard Dumpster

Dimensions: approximately 14 ft long × 8 ft wide × 4 ft high. A solid middle ground for medium cleanouts, carpet removal, and moderate renovation projects. Popular with Darlington homeowners doing seasonal purges or single-room remodels.

20 Yard Dumpster

Dimensions: approximately 22 ft long × 8 ft wide × 4 ft high. The most popular size across Darlington County. Handles kitchen and bathroom gut-outs, deck removals, full-floor flooring replacement, and mid-size estate cleanouts. Fits in standard residential driveways with room to spare.

30 Yard Dumpster

Dimensions: approximately 22 ft long × 8 ft wide × 6 ft high. Built for full home remodels, large roofing tear-offs, and major cleanouts. Common on Darlington County construction sites and larger residential projects. The taller sidewalls mean more capacity without a bigger footprint.

40 Yard Dumpster

Dimensions: approximately 22 ft long × 8 ft wide × 8 ft high. The largest roll-off available — reserved for major construction, commercial demolition, and large-scale property cleanouts. Requires adequate clearance and a firm, level surface. Contractors working near the Raceway or on commercial projects along US-52 often need this size.

Local Regulations

Permits & Regulations in Darlington, SC

Dumpster Placement Permits

If your dumpster is placed on your own property — driveway, yard, or private parking area — no placement permit is typically required in Darlington. However, if you need to place the container on a public street, sidewalk, or right-of-way, you should contact the City of Darlington or Darlington County to check if a permit is needed.

Building and Construction Permits

The City of Darlington requires permits for any construction, repair, or demolition work valued over $200 within city limits. This includes renovation projects that generate dumpster-worthy debris. Each contractor on a job needs a city business license and must submit a contract to the Codes Enforcement Office.

Darlington Codes Enforcement
400 Pearl Street, Darlington, SC
Phone: (843) 398-4029
Building Inspector: Sheila Jones (ext. 106)

Historic District Restrictions

If your property is in one of Darlington's historic districts or listed on the National Register of Historic Places, you'll need approval from the Historic Landmarks Commission before making exterior changes. An Application for a Certificate of Appropriateness is required (fee: $65). If you're only making repairs or painting without changing the appearance, submit an Application of Exemption at no cost. Your dumpster provider can advise on placement that won't impact historic features.

County-Level Rules

For properties in unincorporated Darlington County (outside city limits), contact Darlington County Codes Enforcement at (843) 398-4011 at 1 Public Square, Room 303, Darlington, SC 29532.

Delivery Tips

Delivery & Placement Tips for Darlington

Placement Requirements

Roll-off trucks need approximately 60 feet of straight-line space and 23 feet of vertical clearance to safely place a dumpster. Choose a firm, level surface — concrete driveways work best. Avoid soft ground, especially during wet weather common in the Pee Dee region, as the container's weight can damage lawns and create ruts.

Protecting Your Driveway

Dumpsters are heavy, even before you fill them. Place plywood boards (at least ¾-inch thick) under the contact points to distribute weight and prevent cracking or scraping on your driveway surface. Your provider may supply boards — ask when you confirm your delivery.

Before Delivery Day

Move vehicles, bicycles, and any obstacles out of the delivery path. Trim overhanging tree branches that might obstruct the truck. If a gate needs to be opened, make sure someone is available to let the driver in. You don't have to be home during delivery as long as the area is accessible and clear.

Loading Tips

Load heavy items first (concrete, dirt, lumber) and spread them evenly across the bottom. Walk large items in through the swinging rear door. Fill gaps with smaller debris. Never stack materials above the sidewalls — overfilled containers can't be transported safely and your provider may charge an additional fee.

What Goes In

What You Can & Cannot Throw in a Darlington SC Dumpster

Generally Accepted

  • Household junk, furniture, and appliances (doors removed from refrigerators)
  • Construction and demolition debris (drywall, lumber, siding)
  • Roofing shingles and underlayment
  • Concrete, brick, and stone (note: weight charges may apply)
  • Yard waste, brush, and tree limbs
  • Carpet, tile, and flooring
  • General household cleanout items

Not Accepted (Prohibited Items)

  • Hazardous materials (paint, solvents, chemicals, pesticides)
  • Batteries and electronics (e-waste)
  • Tires (must be disposed of at the Darlington County Landfill directly)
  • Medical waste and pharmaceuticals
  • Asbestos-containing materials
  • Flammable liquids and propane tanks
  • Household garbage in bags (some providers restrict this)

When in doubt, ask your provider before loading. Prohibited items can result in additional disposal fees or rejection at the landfill. For specialty waste disposal options in Darlington County, see the landfill and recycling section below.

Darlington County operates 13 recycling centers located throughout the county, offering free drop-off for newspapers, plastic bottles (#1 and #2), cardboard, used motor oil (limit 10 gallons per visit), oil filters, and textiles.
