# Luminary Paths — Master Prompt

## Project Overview
Luminary Paths is a nonprofit organization being founded to support families with hospitalized children in the Pee Dee region of South Carolina. The name has been reserved with the SC Secretary of State (120-day protection window).

## Mission
Provide direct support to families of hospitalized children — covering gaps in housing, meals, transportation, and financial emergencies that existing organizations (Ronald McDonald House, etc.) don't fully address.

## Current Status
- **Stage**: Pre-incorporation, validation phase
- **Location**: Florence, South Carolina / Pee Dee region
- **Legal structure**: Targeting Nonprofit Corporation with 501(c)(3) status
- **Primary hospital**: McLeod Regional Medical Center

---

## Phase 1: Foundation (Days 1–30)

### Step 1 — Validate the Need
- Contact McLeod Regional Medical Center pediatric unit (Child Life Specialist or Social Work Department)
- Discovery questions:
  - Where do out-of-town families stay?
  - What financial emergencies come up most?
  - What support exists now?
  - What gaps remain?
- Build to actual need, not assumed need

### Step 2 — Confirm Legal Structure
- **Recommended**: Nonprofit Corporation filing as 501(c)(3)
- Reasons: tax-deductible donations, grant eligibility, pure mission focus, reasonable salary allowed
- NOT a C-Corp or Benefit Corporation

### Step 3 — Draft Founding Documents
1. **Articles of Incorporation** — IRS-required charitable purpose clause, dissolution clause, no private benefit clause
2. **Bylaws** — Board size (start 3, grow to 7–9), meeting requirements, officer roles (President, Secretary, Treasurer)
3. **Conflict of Interest Policy** — IRS requirement
- Hire a SC nonprofit attorney ($500–1,500) — do not DIY the legal language

---

## Phase 2: People (Days 30–60)

### Founding Board (3 minimum for SC incorporation)
| Role | Who to Look For | Why |
|------|----------------|-----|
| Treasurer | CPA or financially literate professional | IRS scrutiny on finances |
| Secretary | Detail-oriented organizer | Meeting minutes, compliance |
| Community Connector | Well-connected Florence resident | Donors, hospitals, media |

### Where to Find Board Members
- Florence Rotary Club, Chamber of Commerce events
- Accountant, attorney, or banker introductions
- LinkedIn search: "nonprofit" + "Florence, SC"

### Key Rules
- Board members cannot be paid (expense reimbursement only)
- Time commitment pitch: 2–4 hours monthly for first year

---

## Phase 3: Filing & Status (Days 60–90)

### Week 1
- File Articles of Incorporation with SC Secretary of State ($25–50)
- Get EIN from IRS (free, online, immediate)

### Weeks 2–4
- Draft bylaws, adopt at first board meeting
- Open nonprofit bank account (requires EIN, Articles, bylaws, board resolution)
- File Charitable Solicitation Registration with SC Secretary of State (required before asking for donations)

### Months 3–6
- File for 501(c)(3) status:
  - **1023-EZ**: If projected revenue under $50K/year — $275 fee, 2–4 month approval
  - **Full 1023**: $600 fee, 6–12 months, required if larger scale

---

## Do NOT Do Yet
- Rent office space
- Hire anyone
- Spend money on branding/website beyond basic domain
- Promise specific services to families until funding mechanism exists
- File 501(c)(3) until board is established and bylaws are drafted
