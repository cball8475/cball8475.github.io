Local Regulations

Permits & Regulations in Marion County, SC

Dumpster Placement

Placing a dumpster on private property typically requires no placement-specific permit. For public street or right-of-way placement, check with the City of Marion or Marion County.

Marion County Building Permits

Marion County requires a building permit for any project on commercial or residential buildings where the value exceeds $1,000 and/or the work area exceeds 200 square feet. To obtain a permit, you'll need a zoning compliance permit (issued Tuesdays and Thursdays only), a signed contract between contractor and homeowner, and a current Marion County business license for contractors. The building department only accepts cash or check.

Delivery Tips

Delivery & Placement Tips for Marion

Placement Requirements

Roll-off trucks need approximately 60 feet of straight-line space and 23 feet of vertical clearance. Choose a firm, level surface. Marion County has many rural properties with gravel or dirt driveways — confirm with your provider whether these surfaces can support a loaded dumpster.

Protecting Your Driveway

Place plywood boards (at least ¾-inch thick) under the contact points to prevent cracking on paved surfaces. For gravel driveways, the container will sink slightly under load — this is normal and your provider can advise on the best placement.

Marion County-Specific Tips

Many Marion County properties are accessed by longer driveways or private roads. Make sure your provider knows the exact delivery location and any access limitations — narrow bridges, low-hanging power lines, or soft shoulders. For properties near the Great Pee Dee River or in flood-prone areas, avoid placing dumpsters in low-lying spots during wet weather.

Loading Tips

Load heavy items first, spread evenly. Walk large items through the rear door. Fill gaps with smaller debris. Never stack above sidewalls — overfilled containers can't be safely transported and may incur extra fees.

What Goes In

What You Can & Cannot Throw in a Marion SC Dumpster

Generally Accepted

  • Household junk, furniture, and appliances (doors removed from refrigerators)
  • Construction and demolition debris (drywall, lumber, siding)
  • Roofing shingles and underlayment
  • Concrete, brick, and stone (weight charges may apply)
  • Yard waste, brush, and tree limbs
  • Carpet, tile, and flooring
  • General household cleanout items

Not Accepted (Prohibited Items)

  • Hazardous materials (paint, solvents, chemicals, pesticides)
  • Batteries and electronics (e-waste)
  • Tires (contact Marion County Landfill for proper disposal)
  • Medical waste and pharmaceuticals
  • Asbestos-containing materials
  • Flammable liquids and propane tanks
  • Household garbage in bags (some providers restrict this)

When in doubt, ask your provider before loading. Prohibited items can result in additional disposal fees.
