What Affects the Price?

1. Debris Type

Standard household junk and construction debris fall within the prices above. Heavy materials like concrete, brick, and dirt cost more because they fill weight limits faster. Expect 20-30% higher pricing for heavy debris loads.

2. Rental Duration

Standard rental periods in Florence are 7-14 days. Need it longer? Most providers charge $5-$15 per extra day. If you're flexible on timeline, ask for the maximum included rental period.

3. Weight Overage

Every dumpster has a weight limit (usually 2-8 tons depending on size). Going over costs $40-$75 per extra ton. The easiest way to avoid overages: be honest about your debris type when requesting a quote.

4. Delivery Distance

Providers within Florence County typically include delivery in the base price. For outer Pee Dee communities like Dillon, Mullins, or Sumter, there may be a fuel surcharge of $25-$50.

Tips to Save Money on Dumpster Rental in Florence

  • Size up, not down — a second dumpster delivery costs $200-400 more than upgrading one size from the start.
  • Separate heavy debris — keep concrete/dirt in a separate small container to avoid weight overages on your main dumpster.
  • Get multiple quotes — local Florence providers (Better Bins, Diesel Dumpster, Bush's Containers) often beat national companies by $50-$100.
  • Book midweek — weekends are busiest. Midweek delivery often has better availability and sometimes lower rates.
  • Fill efficiently — break down large items, stack flat materials, and fill from the back using the swing door.

Florence SC vs. National Average

Good news for Florence County residents: local dumpster rental pricing runs 10-20% below the national average. The national average for a 20-yard dumpster is about $450-$500, while Florence SC averages $300-$450. Lower landfill fees and shorter haul distances keep local prices competitive.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest dumpster rental in Florence SC?
10-yard dumpsters start around $250 from local Florence providers. Prices vary by provider and project type. Submit a free quote request to compare local pricing.
Are there hidden fees with dumpster rental?
Common add-on fees include weight overage charges, extended rental fees, and special debris surcharges for items like concrete or tires. Always ask about the total cost including disposal fees when getting a quote.
Is it cheaper to rent a dumpster or haul it yourself?
For most projects, a dumpster rental is more cost-effective. A 20-yard dumpster replaces 6+ pickup truck trips to the landfill. When you factor in gas, dump fees ($50-75 per load at Florence County landfill), and your time, a dumpster typically saves $200-400.
