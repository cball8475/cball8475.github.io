Picking the right size saves money — especially in Dillon County where delivery distances can make a second haul more expensive. Here's how each size maps to common projects:

10 Yard Dumpster

Approximately 12 ft × 8 ft × 3.5 ft. Ideal for single-room cleanouts, garage decluttering, and small bathroom remodels. Fits easily in most Dillon residential driveways.

15 Yard Dumpster

Approximately 14 ft × 8 ft × 4 ft. Great for medium cleanouts, carpet removal, and moderate renovation projects. A solid choice for seasonal cleanup or preparing a property for sale.

20 Yard Dumpster

Approximately 22 ft × 8 ft × 4 ft. The most popular size for Dillon County homeowners. Handles kitchen gut-outs, deck removals, and mid-size estate cleanouts. Fits standard residential driveways.

30 Yard Dumpster

Approximately 22 ft × 8 ft × 6 ft. Built for full home remodels, large roofing tear-offs, and major cleanouts. Common on Dillon County construction sites and commercial projects along the I-95 corridor.

40 Yard Dumpster

Approximately 22 ft × 8 ft × 8 ft. The largest available — for major construction, commercial demolition, and large-scale property cleanouts. Requires adequate clearance and firm, level ground.

Local Regulations

Permits & Regulations in Dillon, SC

Dumpster Placement

Placing a dumpster on private property typically requires no placement-specific permit. For public street or right-of-way placement, check with the City of Dillon or Dillon County.

City of Dillon Building Permits

The City of Dillon requires a building permit before any construction, remodeling, demolition, or alteration work within city limits. Roofing, siding, and pool installations each require separate permits. Obtaining the permit is the contractor's responsibility — not the homeowner's. All contractors must be bonded, licensed, and hold a City of Dillon business license before purchasing a permit.

Delivery Tips

Delivery & Placement Tips for Dillon

Placement Requirements

Roll-off trucks need approximately 60 feet of straight-line space and 23 feet of vertical clearance. Choose a firm, level surface. Dillon County has many properties with gravel or dirt driveways — confirm with your provider whether these surfaces can support a loaded dumpster.

Protecting Your Driveway

Place plywood boards (at least ¾-inch thick) under the contact points to prevent cracking on paved surfaces. For gravel driveways, the container will settle slightly — this is normal.

Dillon-Specific Tips

If your property is near I-95 or in the commercial corridor along US-301, confirm with your provider about any delivery timing restrictions during peak traffic hours. For properties accessed by longer private roads in rural Dillon County, make sure the driver knows the exact route — narrow bridges, soft shoulders, and low-hanging power lines can create access issues for roll-off trucks.

Loading Tips

Load heavy items first, spread evenly across the bottom. Walk large items through the rear door. Fill gaps with smaller debris. Never stack above sidewalls — overfilled containers can't be safely transported and may incur extra fees.

What Goes In

What You Can & Cannot Throw in a Dillon SC Dumpster

Generally Accepted

  • Household junk, furniture, and appliances (doors removed from refrigerators)
  • Construction and demolition debris (drywall, lumber, siding)
  • Roofing shingles and underlayment
  • Concrete, brick, and stone (weight charges may apply)
  • Yard waste, brush, and tree limbs
  • Carpet, tile, and flooring
  • General household cleanout items

Not Accepted (Prohibited Items)

  • Hazardous materials (paint, solvents, chemicals, pesticides)
  • Batteries and electronics (e-waste)
  • Tires (contact the Dillon County Landfill for proper disposal)
  • Medical waste and pharmaceuticals
  • Asbestos-containing materials
  • Flammable liquids and propane tanks
  • Household garbage in bags (some providers restrict this)

When in doubt, ask your provider before loading. Prohibited items can result in additional disposal fees.
